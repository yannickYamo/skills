## Bet Categories

Use these categories to ensure coverage across the marketing mix:

### Messaging Bets
Test which narratives, value propositions, and frames resonate with target audiences.

**Example hypotheses:**
- "Sovereignty narrative outperforms economics narrative for [segment]"
- "'Proven/reliable' positioning beats 'innovative/advanced' for [persona]"
- "Problem-first messaging converts better than product-first"

### Channel Bets
Test where to invest time and budget for maximum pipeline impact.

**Example hypotheses:**
- "Executive LinkedIn content drives more pipeline than event sponsorship"
- "Self-service ROI calculator is highest-converting asset"
- "Site visits convert better than any digital touchpoint"

### ABM Motion Bets
Test engagement strategies and account prioritization approaches.

**Example hypotheses:**
- "Contact-level ads outperform account-level targeting"
- "Reference customer case study unlocks [geography] pipeline"
- "Warm outbound within 48 hours of content engagement converts 5x cold"

### Product Launch Bets
Test launch strategies, timing, and activation approaches.

**Example hypotheses:**
- "Tiered messaging by persona increases launch engagement"
- "Pre-announce period builds more pipeline than big-bang launch"
- "Segment A requires different brand voice than Segment B"

## Bet Scoring Rubric

When reviewing bets, use this rubric:

| Signal | Interpretation | Action |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| Success criteria met early | Strong signal | Amplify: increase investment |
| Trending toward success | Positive signal | Continue: maintain course |
| Flat / inconclusive | Weak signal | Adjust: modify test parameters |
| Trending toward kill condition | Negative signal | Prepare to pivot |
| Kill condition met | Failed hypothesis | Kill: reallocate resources |
